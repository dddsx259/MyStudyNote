# Social Group: Foreign/Migrant Domestic Helpers, M/FDHs

# 1. Effects
## Pros (positive effects)
### 1.1 Economic empowerment and upward mobility for families
+ **Statement**: FDHs earn more money than in hometown, which allows them to support their families and improve their living standards.
+ **Data and arguments**:
  1. The legal minimum wage of FDHs is around [HKD5,100](https://www.news.gov.hk/eng/2025/09/20250929/20250929_163205_842.html) (September 2025, from news.gov.hk), and the average wage is around [HKD5,430](https://www.helperplace.com/blog/domestic-helper-salary-hong-kong) (2026, from helperplace.com). As a comparison:
    + The median monthly wage of local workers in Hong Kong is around [HKD26,500](https://www.salaryhk.com/blog/hong-kong-salary-trends-2026.html) (2026, from salaryhk.com).
    + The average monthly wage in the Philippines is around [PHP13,000](https://wage.is/philippines/)(2026, from wage.is), which is around HKD1,713.
    + The average monthly wage in Indonesia is around [Rp3,500,000](https://wage.is/indonesia/) (2026, from wage.is), which is around HKD1,611.
  2. According to the data from the Central Bank of the Philippines, remittances from **overseas Filipino workers (OFWs)** reached around [USD356.3 billion](https://gulfnews.com/world/asia/philippines/philippine-remittances-hit-record-3563b-in-2025-1.500445849) in 2025, which accounts for around $\frac{356.3b}{4941.6b}\approx 7.2\%$ of the country's GDP. 
+ **Theoretical support**:
  1. **Global labor market theory**: Labor is a commodity that can be traded across borders, and individuals can maximize their income through migration.
  2. **Neoliberal subjectivity**: FDHs are seen as "rational economic agents" who actively choose to work abroad to improve their lives, reflecting the "agency" that globalization provides.
  3. **Global Care Chains**: The labor of care is transferred from women in wealthy countries to women in poorer countries, creating an unequal transfer of "emotional labor".

### 1.2 Digital/Technological Empowerment
+ **Statement**:
  + In the past, FDHs were often isolated and had limited access to information and support. They can't easily find jobs before coming to Hong Kong, and the money they earned can't be easily sent back to their home countries.
  + But with the development of digital technology, FDHs can now easily find job opportunities through online platforms, and they can also use digital financial services to send money back home more conveniently and with lower fees.
+ **Data and arguments**:
  1. Online platforms such as [HelperPlace, 500,000 Users](https://www.helperplace.com/) and [technic 得成, 200,000 Users](https://technic.com.hk/zh/) have made it easier for FDHs to find job opportunities in Hong Kong. These platforms provide a wide range of job listings, allowing FDHs to compare different employers and choose the best fit for their needs.
  2. Around $90$ billion HKD is sent back to the FDHs' home countries by remittances every year.
  3. FDHs can use internet to connect with their families and friends online, learn new skills and access information about their rights and support services. 
+ **Theoretical support**:
  + [Network Society](https://books.google.com.hk/books?hl=zh-CN&lr=&id=FihjywtjTdUC&oi=fnd&pg=PT4&dq=Manuel+Castells+Network+Society&ots=l5_p2TBNa-&sig=OOnUgPNZyFz6indOmnCBBWSWmjg&redir_esc=y#v=onepage&q&f=false)(Manuel Castells): The globalization is driven by the development of digital technology, which has transformed the way people communicate, work, and live. FDHs can use digital technology to connect with their families and friends online, learn new skills and access information about their rights and support services.
  + Digital Divide Theory: While digital technology offers new opportunities, there is a risk of exacerbating inequalities if FDHs lack access to digital tools or skills.

### 1.3 Globalization of right protecting
+ **Statement**: Globalization make the right protection of FDHs not only the responsibility of the Hong Kong government, but also the international community. This can lead to better protection of their rights and interests.
+ **Data and arguments**:
    1. The Hong Kong government has implemented various policies to protect the rights of FDHs, such as the Standard Employment Contract, which sets out the rights and obligations of both employers and employees. However, there are still many cases of abuse and exploitation of FDHs, and the government has been criticized for not doing enough to protect their rights.
    2. There are many NGOs like [Justice Centre Hong Kong](https://www.justicecentre.org.hk) and international organizations that advocate for the rights of FDHs, such as the [International Labour Organization (ILO)](https://www.ilo.org/global/topics/domestic-workers/lang--en/index.htm) and the [Migrant Forum in Asia](https://mfasia.org/). These organizations can provide support and resources for FDHs, and can also put pressure on the Hong Kong government to improve their policies and practices.
    3. The digital media s.t. [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/groups/FDHsupportgroup/) and [X](https://x.com/HELPhk) and tradictional media like [RTHK](https://news.rthk.hk/rthk/ch/component/k2/1678788-20240929.htm) and [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-49728015) can also play a role in raising awareness about the issues faced by FDHs and advocating for their rights.
+ **Theoretical support**:
  + **Global Governance**: Subject to international labor standards and human rights norms.
