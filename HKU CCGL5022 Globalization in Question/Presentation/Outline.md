## Pros (positive effects)-CJH
### 2.1 Economic empowerment and upward mobility for families
+ **Statement**: FDHs earn more money than in hometown, which allows them to support their families and improve their living standards.
+ **Data and arguments**:
  2. The legal minimum wage of FDHs is around [HKD5,100](https://www.news.gov.hk/eng/2025/09/20250929/20250929_163205_842.html) (September 2025, from news.gov.hk), and the average wage is around [HKD5,430](https://www.helperplace.com/blog/domestic-helper-salary-hong-kong) (2026, from helperplace.com). As a comparison:
    + The median monthly wage of local workers in Hong Kong is around [HKD26,500](https://www.salaryhk.com/blog/hong-kong-salary-trends-2026.html) (2026, from salaryhk.com).
    + The average monthly wage in the Philippines is around [PHP13,000](https://wage.is/philippines/)(2026, from wage.is), which is around HKD1,713.
    + The average monthly wage in Indonesia is around [Rp3,500,000](https://wage.is/indonesia/) (2026, from wage.is), which is around HKD1,611.
  2. According to the data from the Central Bank of the Philippines, remittances from **overseas Filipino workers (OFWs)** reached around [USD356.3 billion](https://gulfnews.com/world/asia/philippines/philippine-remittances-hit-record-3563b-in-2025-1.500445849) in 2025, which accounts for around $\frac{356.3b}{4941.6b}\approx 7.2\%$ of the country's GDP. 
+ **Theoretical support**:
  2. **Global labor market theory**: Labor is a commodity that can be traded across borders, and individuals can maximize their income through migration.
  2. **Neoliberal subjectivity**: FDHs are seen as "rational economic agents" who actively choose to work abroad to improve their lives, reflecting the "agency" that globalization provides.
  3. **Global Care Chains**: The labor of care is transferred from women in wealthy countries to women in poorer countries, creating an unequal transfer of "emotional labor".

### 1.2 Digital/Technological Empowerment
+ **Statement**:
  + In the past, FDHs were often isolated and had limited access to information and support. They can't easily find jobs before coming to Hong Kong, and the money they earned can't be easily sent back to their home countries.
  + But with the development of digital technology, FDHs can now easily find job opportunities through online platforms, and they can also use digital financial services to send money back home more conveniently and with lower fees.
+ **Data and arguments**:
  2. Online platforms such as [HelperPlace, 500,000 Users](https://www.helperplace.com/) and [technic 得成, 200,000 Users](https://technic.com.hk/zh/) have made it easier for FDHs to find job opportunities in Hong Kong. These platforms provide a wide range of job listings, allowing FDHs to compare different employers and choose the best fit for their needs.
  2. Around $90$ billion HKD is sent back to the FDHs' home countries by remittances every year.
  3. FDHs can use internet to connect with their families and friends online, learn new skills and access information about their rights and support services. 
+ **Theoretical support**:
  + [Network Society](https://books.google.com.hk/books?hl=zh-CN&lr=&id=FihjywtjTdUC&oi=fnd&pg=PT4&dq=Manuel+Castells+Network+Society&ots=l5_p2TBNa-&sig=OOnUgPNZyFz6indOmnCBBWSWmjg&redir_esc=y#v=onepage&q&f=false)(Manuel Castells): The globalization is driven by the development of digital technology, which has transformed the way people communicate, work, and live. FDHs can use digital technology to connect with their families and friends online, learn new skills and access information about their rights and support services.
  + Digital Divide Theory: While digital technology offers new opportunities, there is a risk of exacerbating inequalities if FDHs lack access to digital tools or skills.

### 1.3 Globalization of right protecting
+ **Statement**: Globalization make the right protection of FDHs not only the responsibility of the Hong Kong government, but also the international community. This can lead to better protection of their rights and interests.
+ **Data and arguments**:
    2. The Hong Kong government has implemented various policies to protect the rights of FDHs, such as the Standard Employment Contract, which sets out the rights and obligations of both employers and employees. However, there are still many cases of abuse and exploitation of FDHs, and the government has been criticized for not doing enough to protect their rights.
    2. There are many NGOs like [Justice Centre Hong Kong](https://www.justicecentre.org.hk) and international organizations that advocate for the rights of FDHs, such as the [International Labour Organization (ILO)](https://www.ilo.org/global/topics/domestic-workers/lang--en/index.htm) and the [Migrant Forum in Asia](https://mfasia.org/). These organizations can provide support and resources for FDHs, and can also put pressure on the Hong Kong government to improve their policies and practices.
    3. The digital media s.t. [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/groups/FDHsupportgroup/) and [X](https://x.com/HELPhk) and tradictional media like [RTHK](https://news.rthk.hk/rthk/ch/component/k2/1678788-20240929.htm) and [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-49728015) can also play a role in raising awareness about the issues faced by FDHs and advocating for their rights.
+ **Theoretical support**:
  + **Global Governance**: Subject to international labor standards and human rights norms.


## Cons (negative effects) - DYX
### 1.4 Global Care Chains and Care Drain
+ **Statement**: 
  + Affluent families employ FDHs to take care of their children and elderly, which in essence is a transfer of care work.
  + The FDHs leave their own families.
  + The care work of FDHs is taken over by other more poor demestic workers.
  + In this chain that the care work is transferred, must be a "care drain" in the bottom of the chain.
+ **Data and arguments**:None
+ **Theoretical support**:
  + **Global Care Chains**: 
    + The labor of care is transferred from women in wealthy countries to women in poorer countries, creating an unequal transfer of "emotional labor".

### 1.5 Precarious Employment & Structural Exploitation
| 制度性剥削 | 说明 |
|---|---|
| **强制同住规定** | FDW必须住在雇主家中，导致工作与生活空间无边界，24小时"随时待命"，极易被超时剥削 |
| **"两周规则"（Two-week Rule）** | FDW终止合同后只有14天必须找到新雇主或离开香港，极大限制了她们对虐待的反抗能力 |
| **签证绑定雇主** | FDW的工作签证与特定雇主绑定，更换雇主的谈判能力几乎为零 |
| **最低工资制度** | FDW的最低工资（2024年为HK$4,990/月）远低于香港本地最低工资标准，形成合法的"廉价劳动力" |

**实证数据**（Cheung et al., 2018 的香港调查）：
- **20.5%** 的菲律宾FDW在过去12个月遭受过身体虐待
- **34.4%** 遭受语言/精神虐待
- 施暴者主要是**女性雇主和其子女**
- **16.7%** 的受害者**不报告**，报告者中仅19.4%向正式机构（中介/警察）投诉

### 1.6 Digital Surveillance and Stigmatization
**核心问题**：全球化带来的技术进步对FDW而言是**双刃剑**——雇主利用数字技术加强监控和压迫：

- **家用监控摄像头**：许多雇主在家中安装隐蔽摄像头，24小时监视FDW的日常活动，制造"全景监狱"效应
- Johnson et al. (2019) 的研究发现，这种监控带来了"关怀与控制的性别化悖论"——摄像头本应"确保照顾质量"，实际上削弱了信任关系，降低了FDW提供最佳照顾的能力
- **社交媒体上的歧视与污名化**：FDW在休息日（周日）聚集在公共空间的行为常在社交媒体上被负面标签化，网络上的歧视性言论加剧了她们的"他者化"

## 3. Contributions of Social Groups/Organizations in Helping FDWs
### 3.1 International Level -CJH
- **International Labour Organization (ILO)**: The [ILO](https://www.ilo.org) has established the [Domestic Workers Convention (C189)](https://normlex.ilo.org/dyn/nrmlx_en/f?p=NORMLEXPUB:12100:0::NO::P12100_INSTRUMENT_ID%2CP12100_LANG_CODE:2551460%2Cen) to set international labor standards for domestic workers, including FDWs. The convention aims to ensure that domestic workers have the same rights and protections as other workers, such as fair wages, reasonable working hours, and protection from abuse and exploitation.
- **CEDAW (Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women)**: [CEDAW](https://www.ohchr.org/en/instruments-mechanisms/instruments/convention-elimination-all-forms-discrimination-against-women) has provided recommendations to improve the treatment of FDWs in Hong Kong, including the abolition of the "two-week rule" and allowing for residency rights conversion.
- **United Nations**: The UN has highlighted the issues faced by FDWs in Hong Kong and has called on the Hong Kong government to take action to protect their rights and improve their working conditions. The UN has also emphasized the importance of international cooperation in addressing the challenges faced by FDWs and ensuring their rights are respected globally.
