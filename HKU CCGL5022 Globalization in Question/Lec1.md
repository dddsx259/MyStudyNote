+ What is globalization?
  + A process of **increasing interdependence** between people, territories and organizations in the **economic, political and cultural domains** --(Verger et al.2016).
  + A set of processes by which the world is rapidly being **integrated into one economic space** via increased international trade, the internationalization of production and financial markets, the internationalization of a commodity culture promoted by an increasingly networked global telecommunications system”. --(Gibson-Graham, 2006).
+ Dimensions of Globalization
  + Economic
  + Political
  + Cultural
+ Major Players of G
  + Nation-states
  + Firms (transnational corporations)
  + International governmental organizations (**IGOs**)
  + International Non-Governmental Organizations (**INGOs**)
  + Other associations and individuals
+ Globalization has always been **adaptive**
+ Period of Globalization:
  + hyper-globalization:
    + Start in 1980s
    + Influence of market ideology
      + Margaret Thatcher in the UK
      + Ronald Reagan in the US
    + Characteristics: 
      + shareholder capitalism(股东资本主义)
      + deregulation(放宽管制)
      + privatization(私有化)
  + De-globalization(逆全球化)
    + Three seismic events:
      + Admittance of China to the World Trade Organization (WTO) in 2001 (world’s factory)
      + Financial crisis in 2008
      + COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 (overreliance on China)
