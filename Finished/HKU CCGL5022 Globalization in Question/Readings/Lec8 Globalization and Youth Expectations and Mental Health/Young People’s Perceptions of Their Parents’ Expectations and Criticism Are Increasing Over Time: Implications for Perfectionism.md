### 文章分析与归纳

#### 中文分析

**文章标题**: 《年轻人对父母期望和批评的感知随时间增加：对完美主义的影响》

**主要内容**:
- **背景**: 近期研究表明，美国、英国和加拿大的年轻人中，自我导向、他人导向和社会规定型完美主义正在上升。一个可能的原因是，日益加剧的竞争和个人主义迫使父母采取焦虑、过度参与和/或过度控制的育儿方式。
- **研究方法**: 通过两项元分析，研究了过度的父母期望和严厉的父母批评是否与完美主义相关（研究1），以及这些感知到的育儿实践是否在美国、加拿大和英国大学生中随时间变化（研究2）。
- **研究结果**:
  - **研究1**: 元分析发现，父母期望和父母批评对自我导向和他人导向完美主义有小到中等的正向加权效应，对社会规定型完美主义有大的正向加权效应。
  - **研究2**: 使用跨时间元分析，发现1989年至2019年间，大学生对父母期望和父母批评的平均水平线性增加。
- **结论**: 在日益加剧的竞争、个人主义、经济不平等和学业压力的社会背景下，父母期望和批评的增加是迄今为止对完美主义上升的最合理解释。

**行文逻辑**:
1. **引言**: 介绍完美主义的多维性和其在现代社会中的重要性。
2. **文献综述**: 回顾完美主义的理论模型，特别是Hewitt和Flett的多维模型。
3. **研究目的**: 阐述研究的两个主要目标：一是检验父母期望和批评与完美主义的关系，二是探讨这些育儿实践是否随时间变化。
4. **研究方法**: 详细描述两项元分析的方法，包括文献搜索、纳入标准、数据分析等。
5. **研究结果**: 报告两项研究的主要发现，包括效应大小、异质性分析和发表偏倚检验。
6. **讨论**: 解释研究结果的意义，讨论父母期望和批评增加的可能原因，并提出未来研究的方向。
7. **结论**: 总结研究的主要发现，强调父母期望和批评对完美主义上升的影响。

**全球化相关理论**:
- **新自由主义**: 文章指出，自20世纪70年代末以来，全球范围内转向新自由主义政策，导致市场竞争加剧、个人主义盛行和经济不平等扩大。这些社会变化对育儿方式产生了深远影响。
- **经济不平等**: 研究发现，收入不平等与父母期望的增加有关。这表明，经济不平等不仅是社会问题，也是家庭内部育儿实践变化的重要因素。
- **文化差异**: 虽然研究主要集中在北美和英国的年轻人，但文章提到，不同文化背景下的完美主义水平和相关因素可能存在差异，未来研究应考虑这些文化差异。

#### 英文 Analysis

**Article Title**: "Young People’s Perceptions of Their Parents’ Expectations and Criticism Are Increasing Over Time: Implications for Perfectionism"

**Main Content**:
- **Background**: Recent evidence shows rising self-oriented, other-oriented, and socially prescribed perfectionism among young people in the United States, United Kingdom, and Canada. One reason for this increase may be that rising competitiveness and individualism are leading parents to engage in anxious, overly involved, and/or overly controlling forms of parenting.
- **Research Methods**: Two meta-analyses were conducted to examine whether excessive parental expectations and harsh parental criticism are correlated with perfectionism (Study 1) and whether these perceived practices are changing over time among American, Canadian, and British college students (Study 2).
- **Results**:
  - **Study 1**: Meta-analyses found small-to-moderate positive mean weighted effects of parental expectations and parental criticism on self-oriented and other-oriented perfectionism, and large positive mean weighted effects on socially prescribed perfectionism.
  - **Study 2**: Using cross-temporal meta-analysis, it was found that mean levels of parental expectations and parental criticism had linearly increased between 1989 and 2019 among college students.
- **Conclusion**: With rising competitiveness, individualism, economic inequality, and pressure to excel at school and college as the societal background, increases in parental expectations and parental criticism offer the most plausible explanation for rising perfectionism to date.

**Writing Logic**:
1. **Introduction**: Introduces the multidimensionality of perfectionism and its significance in modern society.
2. **Literature Review**: Reviews theoretical models of perfectionism, particularly Hewitt and Flett's multidimensional model.
3. **Research Objectives**: Outlines the two main objectives of the study: to examine the relationship between parental expectations/criticism and perfectionism, and to explore whether these parenting practices have changed over time.
4. **Methodology**: Describes the methods used in the two meta-analyses, including literature search, inclusion criteria, and data analysis.
5. **Results**: Reports the main findings of both studies, including effect sizes, heterogeneity analyses, and publication bias tests.
6. **Discussion**: Interprets the results, discusses possible reasons for the increase in parental expectations and criticism, and suggests directions for future research.
7. **Conclusion**: Summarizes the main findings and emphasizes the impact of parental expectations and criticism on the rise of perfectionism.

**Globalization-related Theories**:
- **Neoliberalism**: The article points out that since the late 1970s, there has been a global shift towards neoliberal policies, leading to increased market competition, individualism, and economic inequality. These social changes have profoundly influenced parenting practices.
- **Economic Inequality**: The study finds that income inequality is associated with an increase in parental expectations. This suggests that economic inequality is not only a social issue but also a significant factor in changes in parenting practices within families.
- **Cultural Differences**: While the study primarily focuses on young people in North America and the UK, it mentions that there may be cultural differences in the levels and correlates of perfectionism. Future research should consider these cultural differences.

### 总结
文章通过两项元分析，揭示了父母期望和批评与完美主义之间的关系，并探讨了这些育儿实践随时间的变化。研究发现，父母期望和批评的增加是完美主义上升的最合理解释之一。文章强调了新自由主义和经济不平等对育儿方式的影响，并指出了未来研究应考虑的文化差异。
